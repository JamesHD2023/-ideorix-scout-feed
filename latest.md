1. 🤖 The $1.5 billion Anthropic settlement is now final — the largest AI-copyright payout yet

On Monday (20 July) a US federal judge gave final approval to Anthropic's $1.5 billion settlement with authors, ending the Bartz v. Anthropic case over books pirated to train its Claude AI. It covers roughly 500,000 works at about $3,000 each — the biggest copyright recovery in US history. One honest caveat so nobody scrambles: the claim window already closed on 30 March, so this is NOT a "file now" moment — it's the approval that locks the deal in and moves the money to those who filed. The bigger story is the marker it sets: every AI company training on books now has a price tag to point at.
