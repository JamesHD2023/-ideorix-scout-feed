📚 Daily Writer's Scout — Tuesday, 21 July 2026

1. 🤖 Blind test finds listeners rating multi-voice AI narration above a human narrator — while overall appetite for AI audiobooks falls

A study released 15 July (Edison Research at SSRS, commissioned by AI-narration company Spoken — worth knowing who paid) blind-tested over 1,000 US fiction audiobook listeners: the one-click AI multi-cast version of a sci-fi thriller out-scored a professional single human narrator on favourability (61% v 53%), perceived quality and engagement, and 61% of listeners thought the AI version WAS human. The honest other half: separate Edison research with the Audio Publishers Association finds listeners LESS willing to consider AI-narrated audiobooks in 2026 than they were in 2025 — so the stated resistance is rising even as blind preference shifts. For indie authors weighing audiobook costs, that's the whole debate in one pair of findings.

🔗 https://podnews.net/press-release/edison-spoken

💬 Be honest: if a blind sample fooled you too, would you put an AI-narrated edition of your book on sale — and would you disclose it on the cover page?


2. Kindle Unlimited's June numbers are out: the fund slips from May's record and the page rate eases

Amazon's June KDP Select fund figure, posted to the KDP forum in the last week on its usual mid-month schedule, comes in around $67.0 million — down from May's record $70.3 million — with the US per-page rate easing to roughly $0.0046 from about $0.0049 in May. One month is weather, not climate, but KU authors price series and ad spend off this number, so the direction is worth watching against your own page reads.

🔗 https://www.writtenwordmedia.com/kdp-global-fund-payouts/

💬 KU authors: did your June page reads hold up while the rate slipped, or are you seeing softer read-through too?


3. 🎬 PAGE Awards quarterfinalists are out — top 10% of more than 10,500 scripts

The PAGE International Screenwriting Awards announced its 2026 quarterfinalists on 15 July, the top tenth of a record entry pool drawn from the US and more than 80 countries. If you entered, the list is live now; semifinalists come next month. If you didn't, the pool size itself is the story — screenwriting contests keep growing as a proving ground while traditional development doors stay narrow.
